DAS/SUMI1: Ink Painting 1

David Moeljadi, winter semester and summer semester 2019-2020

Tue 18:30–20:00; Křížkovského 14, KC-2.07

​Introduction to ink painting for beginners. Ink painting ("shuimohua" or "suibokuga" or "sumi-e") is one of the traditional Asian art from China that is closely related to culture and society. It is a method of capturing the beauty through tonality and shading achieved by varying the ink density. This dark-light arrangement creates a three-dimensional effect and a sense of depth and distance. No previous experience is required, students will learn the basics of ink painting:

  • Tools and materials (the "four treasures": brush, ink, inkstone, and paper)
  • Various ink painting techniques
  • Basic techniques of painting various objects: "Four Junzi" or "Shikunshi" (plum blossom, orchid, bamboo, and chrysanthemum), flowers, fruits, vegetables, birds, insects, fish/sea creatures, and scenery/natural landscapes
